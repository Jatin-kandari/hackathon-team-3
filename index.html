    CROWD SOURCING is a method of obtaining information, ideas, or services by soliciting contributions from a large group of people, typically through the internet. It harnesses the collective intelligence and skills of the public, often leading to innovative solutions, diverse perspectives, and cost-effective results. Common applications include: Problem Solving: Companies or organizations pose challenges to the public and reward contributions. Creative Input: Platforms like 99designs allow designers to submit work for client projects. Data Collection: Wikipedia is a prime example, where volunteers contribute knowledge and edit articles. Funding: Crowdfunding platforms like Kickstarter enable entrepreneurs to raise money for projects from individual backers.
